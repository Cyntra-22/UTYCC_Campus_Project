Generating Hydrogen Gas by Water Electrolysis Using Brine Electrolyte

Introduction

Renewable energy is energy obtained from the continuous flows of energy occurring in the natural environment, such as solar energy, wind, hydro and energy from biomass. Water Electrolysis is a very simple process that takes water and passes a supply of electricity through it using immersed electrodes to split into positive hydrogen and oxygen ions migrate through the water towards the cathode and anodes respectively, where electron transfers allow for the diatomic H2 and O2 molecules to form at high purity.

Keywords

Renewable Energy, biomass, Electrolysis, electron

Aims and Objective

  • To generate pure hydrogen by water electrolysis at low-cost
  • To study the reactions during water electrolysis
  • To know the advantages & disadvantages of water electrolysis

Introduction

Aluminum-Air battery produce electricity form the reaction of oxygen in the air with Aluminum. Batteries convert chemical energy into electrical energy. They have two electrodes-called a cathode and anode-where chemical reactions that either use of produce electrons take place.

Soap Making by Hot Process

Introduction

Soaps are made by a chemical reaction between fats/oils and sodium hydroxide (“lye”). Chemical Reaction: Fat or Oil + NaOH  Soap + Glycerol
Because soap is made by this reaction, the reaction is sometimes called saponification.

Keywords

Soaps, Oils, Lye, Sodium Hydroxide, Saponification

Aims and Objective

  • To create holistic skincare products using herbs, spices, exotic oils and essential oils-coining the phrase “skin food”
  • To make products by hands, using traditional methods. This means that glycerin remains in the soap and is not extracted as a by-product.
  • To use low-energy manufacturing processes.
  • To make product that does not dry out your skin and are gentle enough to use on your face
  • To use high-quality material materials where possible, organic
  • To use minimal packing following the principle that a product should sell itself
